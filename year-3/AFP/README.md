 1. **Make backups** of all your work. Every year, at least one, and sometimes two, students have their laptops broken or stolen with loss of data. You don't want to be that student. You may use GitLab or GitHub, for example.

 1. Typos, imprecisions, and bugs

    If you find them in the files here (they certainly are or will be
    present), including this one, you can have a go and submit an
    [issue](https://git.cs.bham.ac.uk/afp/afp-learning-2024-2025/-/issues)
    or, more ambitiously, [merge
    request](https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/merge_requests/creating_merge_requests.html). If
    it is relevant we will merge it (and if it isn't, we will
    politely explain).  Any important pull request or issue will be
    reported as a Canvas announcement and on Teams, though, after we
    have discussed it here on gitlab, and feel free do that yourself.

    Your contributions are welcome.  A [contributing guide](files/Resources/CONTRIBUTING.md),
    which was contributed by a former student of this module, explains how to do this.
